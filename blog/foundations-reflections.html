Foundations Reflections

26/07/2021

What are three new things you have learned about yourself and your ego as a result of the core learning?

I have learned that I tend to set high expectations and therefore underestimate myself a lot as a result especially in terms of my programming ability, I learnt that my mindset and mentality are critical in determining how fast I can learn or even if I can learn a new skill, and that having emotional intelligence is highly important in any field of life no matter how it seems to favour traditional intelligence or IQ.

What are the roles of values, empathy and self awareness in learning or programming?

I think that in programming you are constantly faced with challenges, even the easiest problems can create bugs if you aren't paying your full attention, and so therefore you are often dealing with a lot of tedious debugging and frustration. I think that being aware of this is critical in being a consistenly good programmer as it is easy to let your emotions get the better of you and get angry, both at yourself and potential people you are working with too who might be facing similar issues on seemingly straightforward tasks. I think having values is important too as often it can be easy to get lost in the idea that the best programmers are the ones who can write the shortest code, or solve problems the fastest, when in reality I think that programmers who are able to be objective about their ability and self aware are usually the most successful as they are unlikely to be inhibited by their ego.

What has suprised you the most about the core learining?

I was really fascinated with the neuroplasticity and mindset sections especially the research that was put into them, it allowed me to reflect on past endavours in my life and how I might have been successfull or failed, I tended to notice that these mindsets often have correlated with success in my life previously, and it helped me pinpoint why other things may not have worked out as I hoped by evaluating what my mindset was in those moments of learning.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

I think generally just sharing my reflections and ideas with other people was pretty daunting at first (and maybe still is). I do a lot of thinking about myself but never tend to share these ideas with other people which creates several challenges mainly trying to verbalise what I am actually thinking in a way that someone else can understand it properly.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development bootcamp course?

I would say because programming is a field where you are constantly needing to learn and absorb new information, it makes sense to focus not on the information itself but the ability to learn and retain new things comfortably and consistently. This can be an especially hard thing to do because you can always feel like you are a beginner or seeing something for the first time. I think having the tools to cope with these kinds of experiences regularly is a huge benefit in the industry and probably helps a lot with things like mental health as well, and I can imagine would be vital in the longevity of people's careers.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practising programming instead?

I think it feels like a good use of time. I like that it adds a more human aspect to coding whereas in the past it feels like coding is treated more like a maths or science type of subject where all that matters is the data and results. I think that developing the self awareness in any sort of subject is really valuable and understanding what is happening under the hood of your brain when you are trying to learn something new will help with things like getting stuck or not understanding something properly.
