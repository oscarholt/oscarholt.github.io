Identity and Values

25/06/2021

Core Personal Values

My core personal values tend to be centered around growth. To me growing is one of the most important values to me right now as I always feel I have room to learn and improve and enjoy the process of harnessing new skills and honing ones I already have. Part of this involves being humble and adopting a learners mindset which is a big value for me and something I often struggled with in the past.

A decision I have to make many times is when I am teaching kickboxing classes, and a student appears to be struggling with some form of technique or movement. Generally I have to weigh up the value of critiquing them in the hopes that it will lead to their improvement, versus the value of simply encouraging them and trying to raise their confidence which may be more helpful in the long run. Although it does vary from person to person, generally I try to offer advice as this is part of the job I am trying to do. Most of the time the students can take it well, but sometimes the consequences of this are that the student take a small blow to their ego, or be overwhelmed by the new information when they are already grappling with what they currently know. This can often times have the adverse effect and by lowering the students confidence potentially means they may even struggle more with whatever they were trying to do in the first place.

My culture has influenced my values and identity profoundly. My parents always encouraged me to learn and be open minded towards trying new things, but also to be disciplined and practice things well, such as when I took music lessons or other extra-cirricular activities. At my high school there was a large emphasis placed on academic achievement which influenced me to value achievement more generally as a good goal or direction to want to move in. My friends are all a similar way in terms of enjoying the pursuit of learning and bettering themselves, which definitely rubs off on me and engrains the mentality of wanting to grow even further.

Strengths and Limitations

My strengths in my learning and career development would be being open minded, having a good learning mindset, rational thinking and creating and achieving goals. My limitations would be having my standards set too high, impatience and being overly critical of myself and others.

I believe most of these strengths will help me in my learning journey, particularly being enthusiastic about learning and setting and achieving goals. This will intersect with learning obstacles such as having to learn things I have no experience with, having a large workload and managing my own learning.

An example of trying to work productively with others was during my university assignments which involved working on a group project where we were programming an autonomous vehicle. There was resistance and tension when a few group members had disagreements about allegedly plagarising code from friends which was making it difficult to move ahead with the project. At the time I tried to resolve it by talking to each of them individually and explaining the other persons perspective in a way that seemed more neutral so they could each understand the other persons reasons for feeling / acting a certain way. I would say this was somewhat effective as we did manage to complete and pass the project. I would try a slightly different strategy now of mediating a discussion between them as this would hopefully allow for a much clearer resolution of the conflict as they are face to face and can hopefully get a better idea of the issues each person presents. This is because it would not be as much of a band-aid solution, but more of a solution that would work long term and help the group members to work together in the future even outside of the current project.
